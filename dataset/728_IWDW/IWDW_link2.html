Introduction

In the past two decades, the International Workshops on Digital Forensics and Watermarking (IWDW) have consistently gathered researchers from around the world. These workshops serve as a platform for addressing challenges, fostering idea exchange, and sharing the latest advancements and technical expertise in the fields of information hiding and digital forensics. Our researchers also pay close attention to the development of related fields and the security issues they bring forth, such as AI, smart cities, and industrial Internet. Following this tradition, the aim of IWDW 2023 is bring together researchers to discuss their most recent works. IWDW 2023 is held by Qilu University of Technology at Jinan, China, guided and sponsered by IWDW Steering Committee.

Topics of Interest

The topics of interest include but are not limited to:

  • *Authentication, copyright protection, DRM, and forensics watermarking
  • *Artificial Intelligence and Applications
  • *Security of Artificial Intelligence
  • *Security in IOT
  • *Combination of data hiding and cryptography
  • *Fake multimedia forensics and anti-forensics
  • *Information theoretic, stochastic and capacity aspects of data hiding
  • *Large-scale experimental tests and benchmarking
  • *Statistical and perceptual models of multimedia content for multimedia security
  • *Reversible data hiding
  • *Robust perceptual hashing
  • *Security issues in multimedia protection, including attacks and counter-attacks
  • *Steganography and steganalysis (Steganology)
  • *Media source forensics and identification
  • *Cryptography

Each submitted paper will be reviewed by at least 2 reviewers. There will be two prizes: one for the best paper and the other for the best student paper. The proceedings of IWDW 2023 will be published on the Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) by Springer.
